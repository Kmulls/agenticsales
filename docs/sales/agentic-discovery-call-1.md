# AI Discovery Call 1: Pure Discovery Framework

### Enhanced Script & Question Bank

This document expands only on **Call Number One** from Corey Ganim's *Build With AI* mini-assessment playbook. It is designed as a practical, ready-to-use guide for turning conversations into high-conversion discovery sessions (leading to $999+ paid AI audits/implementations at 30–50% rates).

**The call's core goal:** Pure discovery — no prescribing solutions. Ask, listen deeply (prospect should talk 70–80%), quantify pain in their words, identify one high-leverage bottleneck (highest frequency × friction, weighted to their chosen ROI lever: more revenue, more hours back, or happier customers), name it back to them, and book Call 2. This builds massive trust through client-led insight.

It is structured around the 5 core questions, with:

- **Main Question** (direct from framework).
- **Slight Variations** (natural rephrasings for flow, rapport, industry fit, or energy — e.g., for real estate, e-comm, coaching, or service businesses).
- **Quick Follow-up Probes** (2 short, targeted questions for quick depth without derailing — keep these to 1–2 minutes total per main question).
- **Sample Script Section** featuring a very interested prospect (e.g., a busy real estate team leader or entrepreneur excited about AI, giving detailed, engaged responses that reveal pain and vision).

At the end: closing language and tips.

---

## Q1: The Forking Question (Sets the ROI Lens)

**Main Question:** "What matters more to you right now: more revenue, more hours back in your week, or happier customers?"

**Slight Variations:**

- "If you could wave a wand and improve one key area — driving more revenue, reclaiming your time, or boosting customer satisfaction — which would have the biggest impact on your business this quarter?"
- "Between growing top-line revenue, getting hours back, or making clients happier, what's feeling most urgent for you?"
- "What's the top priority when you think about where AI or systems could help most: revenue, time freedom, or customer experience?"

**Quick Follow-up Probes:**

1. "Why that one specifically right now?" (or "What makes that the biggest opportunity?")
2. "How does that tie into your bigger business or personal goals?"

**Sample Script Section (Very Interested Prospect — Real Estate Team Leader):**

**You:** "To ensure we focus on what will actually move the needle, let me start with this: What matters more right now — more revenue, more hours back, or happier customers?"

**Prospect:** "Hands down, more hours back. I'm swamped with operations and can't even think about scaling."

**You:** "Why that one specifically?"

**Prospect:** "Revenue is solid, but I'm working nights and weekends. My team needs more from me as a leader, and I'm burning out — it's unsustainable."

**You:** "How does reclaiming time connect to your bigger goals?"

**Prospect:** "I want to expand to 8–10 agents and focus on luxury listings and coaching. More time would let me do that instead of being stuck in the daily grind. This AI stuff could be the key."

## Q2: The Repetition Question (Identifies High-Frequency Tasks)

**Main Question:** "What's your most repeated weekly task?"

**Slight Variations:**

- "What's the one activity or task you find yourself repeating the most throughout the week?"
- "If you looked at your calendar, what keeps coming up over and over as a time-eater?"
- "What recurring responsibility shows up multiple times every week without fail?"

**Quick Follow-up Probes:**

1. "How frequently does that actually happen — daily, multiple times a day, or a few times weekly?"
2. "Can you walk me through a quick example of what that looks like in practice?"

**Sample Script Section:**

**You:** "With time as your priority, what's your most repeated weekly task?"

**Prospect:** "Lead follow-up and qualification from our website, Zillow, and referrals. It's nonstop."

**You:** "How frequently?"

**Prospect:** "Multiple times daily — easily 20–30 interactions a week that need personal attention."

**You:** "Walk me through a typical example."

**Prospect:** "A lead comes in, I qualify them, answer the same property questions, schedule viewings, send docs… it's repetitive and pulls me away from high-value work every single day."

---

## Q3: The Friction Question (Uncovers Pain Points & Bottlenecks)

**Main Question:** "Where do things stall or slip through the cracks?"

**Slight Variations:**

- "In that process, where do delays, errors, or dropped balls usually happen?"
- "What parts of your workflow tend to get bogged down or fall through the cracks?"
- "Where exactly does friction or inconsistency show up most?"

**Quick Follow-up Probes:**

1. "What's the typical impact when that stall happens?" (e.g., lost deals, stress, rework)
2. "How does that affect your team, clients, or overall momentum?"

**Sample Script Section:**

**You:** "Where do things stall or slip in that lead follow-up process?"

**Prospect:** "Response time and qualification — I can't always reply instantly, and warm leads go cold while I'm at showings."

**You:** "What's the impact when that happens?"

**Prospect:** "Lost revenue from deals that slip away, plus wasted time later chasing bad fits. It's frustrating and costly."

**You:** "How does it affect your team or clients?"

**Prospect:** "My assistant helps but it's inconsistent, so clients complain about slow replies, hurting our reputation. The team feels the pressure too."

---

## Q4: The ROI Anchor / Quantification Question (Makes Pain Concrete)

**Main Question:** "How many hours per week does that take?" + "What's the rough value of that time (yours or the team's)?"

**Slight Variations:**

- "Roughly how much weekly time goes into this, and what would reclaiming it be worth?"
- "Can you quantify that in hours per week and the dollar impact on the business?"
- "What's the real cost in time and opportunity when this task drags on?"

**Quick Follow-up Probes:**

1. "Who else gets pulled into this, and how?"
2. "Beyond hours, what other costs are there (e.g., stress, missed opportunities, errors)?"

**Sample Script Section:**

**You:** "How many hours a week would you say lead follow-up consumes?"

**Prospect:** "Conservatively 12–15 hours. It adds up fast with all the back-and-forth."

**You:** "What value would you assign to that time?"

**Prospect:** "My closing time is worth $200+/hour easily. So we're talking $2,400–$3,000 a week in opportunity cost. Insane when you put it that way."

**You:** "Who else is involved?"

**Prospect:** "My assistant spends several hours on it too, pulling her from marketing and admin."

**You:** "Any other hidden costs?"

**Prospect:** "The constant mental drain and anxiety about dropped leads — it affects my focus and even family time."

---

## Q5: The Magic Wand Question (Surfaces Vision & Emotional Payoff)

**Main Question:** "If this ran itself tomorrow, what would change?"

**Slight Variations:**

- "If that entire process was fully automated and seamless, how would your day-to-day or business look?"
- "Picture it running perfectly on its own — what becomes possible?"
- "If the friction disappeared overnight, what would be different for you and the team?"

**Quick Follow-up Probes:**

1. "What would you personally do with that extra time or bandwidth?"
2. "How would that directly support your top priority around [their lever, e.g., more hours back]?"

**Sample Script Section:**

**You:** "If lead follow-up ran itself tomorrow, what would change?"

**Prospect:** "I'd finally breathe. No more inbox obsession or phone pings interrupting everything."

**You:** "What would you do with that time?"

**Prospect:** "Focus on seller consultations, agent training, and networking events. Maybe even take real vacations without guilt."

**You:** "How would that support getting more hours back and scaling?"

**Prospect:** "It would free me to lead instead of operate — grow the team, close bigger deals, and build the business I envisioned. This feels like exactly the leverage I've been missing with AI."

## Closing Call 1 & Booking Call 2

**Sample Close:**

**You:** "Thank you for sharing all that detail — it's really helpful. The clear bottleneck I'm hearing is the high-frequency, high-friction lead qualification and follow-up process. It's eating 12–15+ hours a week at high opportunity cost, directly blocking your goal of reclaiming time to scale the team. Does that capture it accurately?

Awesome. I'll prepare one targeted prescription for our next call: the right tool/approach, with cost and an immediate first step. How does [specific time, e.g., Tuesday 10am] work for a quick 15-minute follow-up?"

**Prospect (interested):** "Yes, spot on. I'm really excited about this — let's lock in Tuesday."

## Additional Considerations, Nuances & Edge Cases

- **Flow Flexibility:** Questions are sequential but conversational. Use variations to match the prospect's language/energy. If they jump ahead, roll with it and circle back.
- **Active Listening & Reflection:** After each answer, briefly mirror ("It sounds like the repetition is draining because…") to build rapport.
- **Note-Taking:** Capture verbatim phrases for ROI math, bottleneck naming, and Call 2 prep.
- **Time Control:** Target 12–15 minutes. The interested prospect above shows rich responses without needing long detours.
- **Edge Cases:** Multiple bottlenecks? Prioritize the single highest (frequency × friction × lever). Unclear answers? Gentle probes usually clarify. Low engagement? The forking question and magic wand often spark interest. Non-business contexts (e.g., solopreneurs) adapt levers naturally.
- **Why This Works:** Client-owned quantification + vision creates buy-in. Variations add adaptability for different personalities/industries while keeping the framework tight. For a real estate / AI consulting background, this maps perfectly to lead gen, content, or deal workflows.
